Product Information

Most of our beans, rice, and whole grains are naturally gluten-free. However, they are processed in facilities that may also handle wheat products. We recommend checking the specific product packaging for the most accurate information, especially if you have a severe gluten sensitivity or celiac disease.

When stored in a cool, dry place in an airtight container, our dry beans, rice, and grains can last for a very long time, often a year or more. For best quality, we recommend using them within one year of purchase. Always check the "Best By" date on the packaging if available.

Cooking & Storage

Soaking beans before cooking can help reduce cooking time and may also make them easier to digest. You can soak them overnight or use a quick-soak method (boil for 2-3 minutes, then let stand covered for an hour). However, some beans like lentils and split peas generally do not require soaking.

Once opened, transfer any unused dry beans, rice, or grains to an airtight container and store in a cool, dark, and dry place, such as a pantry or cupboard, to maintain freshness and prevent pest infestation.
